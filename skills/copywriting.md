# Copywriting

Write and improve marketing copy for homepages, landing pages, pricing pages, feature pages, and product pages. Create compelling content that converts visitors into customers.

## Triggers

Use this skill when the user requests:
- Writing marketing copy
- Creating landing page content
- Improving conversion rates through copy
- Crafting headlines and taglines
- Writing product descriptions

Keywords: "copy", "copywriting", "landing page", "headline", "tagline", "marketing", "conversion", "CTA", "value proposition"

## Features

- **Headline and Tagline Writing**: Attention-grabbing opening statements
- **Value Proposition Crafting**: Clear articulation of benefits
- **Call-to-Action Optimization**: Compelling action prompts
- **Benefit-Focused Messaging**: Customer-centric communication
- **Conversion-Oriented Structure**: Layouts that drive action

## Copywriting Formulas

### AIDA (Attention, Interest, Desire, Action)
1. **Attention**: Grab with a bold headline
2. **Interest**: Engage with relevant information
3. **Desire**: Create want through benefits
4. **Action**: Prompt with clear CTA

### PAS (Problem, Agitation, Solution)
1. **Problem**: Identify the pain point
2. **Agitation**: Make the problem feel urgent
3. **Solution**: Present your product as the answer

### BAB (Before, After, Bridge)
1. **Before**: Current painful state
2. **After**: Desired future state
3. **Bridge**: Your product connects them

## Headline Formulas

```
[Number] Ways to [Achieve Desired Outcome] Without [Common Pain Point]

How [Company/Person] [Achieved Result] in [Timeframe]

The [Adjective] Way to [Desired Outcome]

Stop [Pain Point]. Start [Desired Outcome].

[Desired Outcome] Made Simple

Finally, [Solution] That [Key Benefit]

[Do Something] Like [Aspirational Example]
```

## CTA Best Practices

### Strong CTAs
- Start Your Free Trial
- Get Started Free
- See It In Action
- Claim Your Spot
- Join [Number] Happy Customers

### Weak CTAs (Avoid)
- Submit
- Click Here
- Learn More (overused)
- Sign Up

### CTA Optimization Tips
1. Use first person: "Start My Free Trial" vs "Start Your Free Trial"
2. Add urgency when appropriate: "Get Started Today"
3. Reduce friction: "No credit card required"
4. Be specific: "Download the Guide" vs "Get It"

## Writing Guidelines

### Voice and Tone
- **Confident**: State benefits directly, avoid hedging
- **Conversational**: Write like you speak
- **Customer-focused**: Use "you" more than "we"
- **Specific**: Use numbers and concrete details

### Common Mistakes
- Feature-focused instead of benefit-focused
- Too much jargon or buzzwords
- Weak or generic headlines
- Missing social proof
- Unclear call to action
- Too much text, not enough white space

## Best Practices

1. **Lead with benefits**: Features tell, benefits sell
2. **Use social proof**: Testimonials, logos, numbers
3. **Create urgency**: Limited time, limited spots, etc.
4. **Reduce risk**: Guarantees, free trials, easy cancellation
5. **Test everything**: Headlines, CTAs, and layouts
